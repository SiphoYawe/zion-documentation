title: Retests and overrides
description: Managing retest attempts for failed PACE tests, the retest cap, and the administrator override

A retest is required whenever a graded test (a PACE test or final test) is scored below the pass threshold. Self tests are ungraded readiness checks and never trigger a retest. Administrators, supervisors, and monitors schedule retests, enter retest scores, and cancel scheduled retests. Administrators grant the override when a student exhausts the retest cap.

## When a retest is required

A failing graded score notifies the student&apos;s centre supervisors, monitors, and administrators that a retest is needed. The pass threshold that determines pass or fail is set per grade band:

| Grade band | Default pass threshold |
|---|---|
| Grades 1-4 | 90% |
| Grades 5-12 | 80% |

These defaults are configurable in **Settings &gt; Grading**. Self tests are excluded from this workflow entirely: they are never moderated and never retested.

## Scheduling a retest

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Retests">
    Navigate to **Grading &gt; Retests** and open the **Failed Tests** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the failed test">
    Locate the student&apos;s failed test. Supervisors and monitors see only tests at their assigned learning centres.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schedule the retest">
    Set a retest date. The date must be today or later; past dates are rejected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    The test moves to the **Pending Retests** tab with its scheduled date.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A test can only be scheduled for retest if it is currently failed and does not already have a retest scheduled.

## Entering a retest score

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Pending Retests">
    Navigate to **Grading &gt; Retests**, then the **Pending Retests** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the scheduled retest">
    Locate the student&apos;s scheduled retest.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the score">
    Enter the new score. Zion creates a new score record chained to the original attempt via `original_attempt_id`, increments the attempt number, and clears the scheduled date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    The retest inherits the original test&apos;s pass threshold and test type, and is routed through moderation the same way the original attempt was (Grade 8 and above by default, unless your school changed the moderation floor).
  </Step>
</Steps>

If the retest score passes, the chain is complete and no further retest is needed. If it fails and attempts remain under the cap, the student can be scheduled for another retest.

## The retest cap

Each retest chain is capped at a maximum number of attempts, including the original test.

- Default: 3 attempts
- Configurable per school in **Settings &gt; Grading**, from 1 to 5 attempts in the settings screen
- The cap applies to the chain as a whole. Once a chain reaches its cap and the latest attempt still failed, entry of a further retest is blocked until an administrator grants an override.

<Warning>
Changes to the retest cap apply to future test chains only. Chains already in progress keep the cap that was in effect when the original test was entered.
</Warning>

## Administrator override for an exhausted cap

When a retest chain hits its cap and the student is still failing, an administrator can grant an override from the Failed Tests table.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the capped test">
    From **Grading &gt; Retests &gt; Failed Tests**, find the test flagged as having reached its retest cap and open **Grant Override**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the override decision">
    Choose one of two decisions:
    - **Authorize another attempt**: raises the cap by one for this chain, allowing one more retest to be scheduled.
    - **Approve progression**: records an intervention plan and lets PACE progression treat the PACE as satisfied without a further passing score.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provide a reason">
    Enter a reason of at least 10 characters. This is required to grant the override.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Zion writes an `admin_override` entry to the audit log.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Cancelling a scheduled retest

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the scheduled retest">
    Locate it in the **Pending Retests** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cancel">
    Cancel the scheduled retest. This clears the scheduled date so the test returns to Failed Tests, where it can be rescheduled or handled with an override if the cap has been reached.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Score correction and cancellation controls

Two separate controls exist for correcting scores after entry, and they apply to different situations.

| Control | Who | When it applies |
|---|---|---|
| Adjust score | Moderator | Correcting a score value before it is finalized and locked, with a mandatory reason of at least 10 characters |
| Correct moderated score | Administrator, supervisor | Correcting a score that has already been finalized and locked (Stage 3), as a documented exception to the lock |
| Cancel retest | Administrator, supervisor, monitor | Cancelling a scheduled retest date before it is taken |

Every adjustment, correction, and cancellation writes a row to the grade audit log, so the history of a score, including who changed it and why, is always available. See [Audit trail](/features/grading/audit-trail) for how to review that history.
