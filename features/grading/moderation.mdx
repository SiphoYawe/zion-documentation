---
title: The three-stage moderation pipeline
description: How internal moderation, external (ACE) moderation, and finalization protect graded PACE scores for Grade 8 and above
icon: "clipboard-check"
---

Graded test scores (PACE tests and final tests) for students at or above the school&apos;s moderation floor, Grade 8 by default, pass through a three-stage moderation pipeline before they count toward averages, progression, and report cards. Self-tests are readiness checks and never enter this pipeline; they finalize the moment a score is entered.

Administrators use this pipeline by default. A school can delegate stage 1 or stage 2 moderation to supervisors or monitors. This page covers the three stages, the moderation queue, SLA tracking and alerts, and how moderator roles are configured.

## The three stages

<Steps>
  <Step title="Stage 1: entered, pending moderation">
    Staff enter a score at Grading &gt; Score Entry. If the student is at or above the moderation floor and the test type is graded (pace_test or final_test), the score is stamped `stage_1_pending` and enters the internal moderation queue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stage 2: internal moderation">
    A moderator reviews the score at Grading &gt; Moderation Queue and either approves it (`stage_2_internal_approved`) or rejects it back to the original grader (`stage_1_rejected`) with a mandatory reason.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stage 3: external (ACE) moderation">
    A second moderator reviews internally-approved scores at Grading &gt; External Moderation and either finalizes the score (`stage_3_external_approved`, the row is locked) or flags it for ACE accreditation-body review (`flagged_for_ace`).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Separation of duties applies at every stage. A moderator cannot approve, reject, or finalize a score they entered themselves, and the stage-2 moderator cannot also perform stage-3 external moderation on the same score.
</Note>

## Stage 1: internal moderation queue

Navigate to **Grading &gt; Moderation Queue**. The queue lists every score in `stage_1_pending`, filterable by learning centre, subject, grade level, and SLA status. Each row shows the student, PACE, entered score, pass or fail indicator, who entered it, entry time, and hours pending.

For each pending score, a moderator has two actions:

### Approve

Approving accepts the score as entered. The score moves to `stage_2_internal_approved` and a `moderation_approved` entry is written to the audit log. A moderator can add an optional note.

### Reject

Rejecting sends the score back to the original grader. This requires a reason of at least 10 characters. The score moves to `stage_1_rejected`, and the original grader (or another staff member at that centre) can resubmit a corrected score from the Rejected Scores section of Score Entry. Resubmission restamps the score back to `stage_1_pending`, restarts the SLA clock, and logs a `score_adjusted` audit entry.

<Warning>
An adjustment or rejection reason under 10 characters is rejected by the form. This is enforced for audit compliance, not a soft suggestion.
</Warning>

<Tip>
Adjusting a score value in place (rather than rejecting it) is also available during moderation. The original score is preserved in the audit log, the new value is applied, and a `score_adjusted` flag is set on the row.
</Tip>

## Stage 2 to Stage 3: external moderation

Navigate to **Grading &gt; External Moderation** to review scores already approved internally. This is where the final ACE-facing decision is made.

| Action | Result |
|---|---|
| **Approve and finalize** | Score moves to `stage_3_external_approved`, `is_locked` is set to true, and a `grade_finalized` audit entry is written. The score now counts toward averages, progression, and report cards. |
| **Flag for ACE review** | Score moves to `flagged_for_ace` and is held out of all finalized-score calculations until the accreditation review is resolved. |

A locked score cannot be changed through normal moderation actions once it reaches `stage_3_external_approved`. Administrators and supervisors have a separate, documented exception, `correctModeratedScore`, to correct an already-locked score when needed; that correction is written to the audit trail like any other change. See [Audit Trail](/features/grading/audit-trail).

## Delegated and configurable moderator roles

By default, only the administrator role can perform internal or external moderation. A school can delegate either stage independently:

- **internal_moderator_role**: can be set to supervisor or monitor to let that role perform stage 1 approvals and rejections.
- **external_moderator_role**: can be set to supervisor or monitor to let that role perform stage 3 finalization and ACE flagging.

Both are configured from the **Moderation Role Settings** card on the Moderation Queue page, administrator-only. Neither can be delegated to student or parent roles.

A delegated moderator&apos;s authority is scoped to the learning centres they are assigned to. They cannot moderate scores at a centre they are not assigned to, and if an administrator removes the delegation, it takes effect immediately on the next queue read, not on a cache delay.

<Note>
A staff member can also gain centre-scoped moderation authority through an individual `grading:moderate` permission grant, independent of the internal_moderator_role or external_moderator_role setting. See [Roles and Permissions](/features/organization/roles-and-permissions).
</Note>

## Moderation queue management

### Reassigning a stuck moderation

An administrator can reassign a pending score to a different active staff member (administrator, supervisor, or monitor) from the queue. Reassignment updates the assigned moderator, writes a `moderation_reassigned` audit entry, and notifies the newly assigned moderator.

### Moderator metrics and history

The moderation queue page shows per-moderator performance metrics and a moderation history. Any moderator can view their own history; administrators can view any moderator&apos;s history.

### Filtering and prioritizing

Filter the queue by learning centre, subject, grade level, or SLA status to divide work across multiple moderators, or to focus on the oldest items first.

## SLA tracking and moderation alerts

Each moderation stage has its own SLA clock, and the internal and external windows are different lengths.

**Internal moderation (stage 1 to stage 2)**, clock starts at score entry:

| Status | Time pending |
|---|---|
| Within SLA | &lt;36 hours |
| Approaching | 36 to 48 hours |
| Overdue | 48 to 72 hours |
| Escalated | &gt;=72 hours |

**External moderation (stage 2 to stage 3)**, clock starts when internal moderation approves the score:

| Status | Time pending |
|---|---|
| Within SLA | &lt;120 hours |
| Approaching | 120 to 168 hours |
| Overdue | 168 to 192 hours |
| Escalated | &gt;=192 hours |

These thresholds are fixed in the system and are not configurable per school.

### Moderation Alerts page

Navigate to **Grading &gt; Alerts** to see active alerts grouped by escalated, overdue, and warning severity, each showing the student, score, and hours pending. An **Acknowledged** tab holds the alert history.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open an alert">
    Review the student, score, and how long it has been pending.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Acknowledge it">
    Click Acknowledge if you have seen the item but cannot process it immediately. The alert moves to the acknowledged list but the underlying score still needs to be moderated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Process the score">
    Go to the relevant moderation queue (internal or external) to approve, reject, or finalize it.
  </Step>
</Steps>
