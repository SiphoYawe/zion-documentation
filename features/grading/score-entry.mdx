title: Entering test scores

---

Monitors, supervisors, and administrators enter PACE test scores from **Grading &gt; Score Entry** (`/grading/entry`). The page has a single-entry tab for one score at a time and a batch-entry tab for entering several scores in one pass.

## Single entry

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the student">
    Search by name or student number. Results filter as you type. Selecting a student shows their full name, student number, grade level, and learning centre.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the PACE">
    The dropdown lists only PACEs assigned to that student, shown as subject and number (for example, Math 1042). If a PACE you expect is missing, check the student&apos;s PACE assignments in Student Management.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the test type">
    Pick pace_test, self_test, or final_test. This choice affects both grading (pace_test and final_test count toward averages and progression; self_test does not) and where the score goes next.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the score and date">
    Score must be a whole number from 0 to 100. Test date must be today or an earlier date, never in the future.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and submit">
    The form shows the computed pass/fail status before you submit. Confirm the details and submit the entry.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Batch entry

The batch tab lets you enter multiple scores in one session instead of repeating the single-entry flow for each student. Each row still runs through the same validation: score range, test date, PACE assignment, and duplicate checks apply per row.

## Pass/fail feedback

As soon as a score is entered, Zion calculates a pass threshold from the student&apos;s grade level and shows pass or fail immediately.

| Grade band | Default pass threshold |
|---|---|
| Grades 1 to 4 | 90% |
| Grades 5 to 12 | 80% |

An administrator can move the grade cutoff between these two bands and change the percentages for each band in grading settings. A failing pace_test or final_test score notifies the student&apos;s centre supervisors, monitors, and administrators that a retest is needed. A failing self_test does not trigger this notification, since self_test is an ungraded readiness check.

## Duplicate detection

Zion blocks a duplicate entry for the same student, PACE, test type, and attempt number. The check runs before submission and is also enforced at the database level, so a duplicate cannot slip through even from concurrent entries.

An entry is not blocked when it is a different test type for the same PACE, a retest (attempt number 2 or higher), or a different term. If you hit a duplicate error, check whether the score was already entered before retrying. To correct an existing score, contact your administrator.

## Where a recorded score goes next

What happens after submission depends on the test type and the student&apos;s grade level:

- **self_test:** Always finalizes immediately. It is never moderated and is excluded from grade averages and PACE progression checks.
- **pace_test or final_test, below the moderation floor (default below Grade 8):** Finalizes immediately and is available for grade calculations and the student&apos;s record.
- **pace_test or final_test, at or above the moderation floor (default Grade 8 and up):** Enters the moderation queue at stage 1 pending. It is not yet finalized and awaits review by an internal moderator.

The moderation floor and the pass-threshold band cutoff are both configurable by an administrator, described in the grading overview.

## Rejected scores

If an internal moderator rejects a stage 1 score, it comes back to the original grader with a rejection reason. Re-entering the corrected score resubmits it to stage 1 pending, and the prior rejection no longer shows once the resubmission is logged. A score that has already cleared moderation and been locked can only be corrected by an administrator or supervisor as a documented exception, not by re-entering it here.

<Warning>
A wrong student or wrong score noticed after submission cannot be self-corrected from the entry form. Contact your administrator, who can adjust the entry and record the correction in the audit trail.
</Warning>
