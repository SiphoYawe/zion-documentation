Zion's grading module records PACE test scores, routes recorded grades for older students through a moderation pipeline, tracks retests for failed tests, and logs every action for ACE accreditation review. Administrators, supervisors, and monitors use it depending on their role and learning centre assignment.

## Ungraded self-tests vs recorded graded tests

A PACE test is entered as one of three types, and the type decides how the score is treated.

| Test type | Purpose | Moderation |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Self test | Mid-PACE readiness check the student takes before the real test | Finalizes immediately, never moderated, excluded from averages and PACE progression checks |
| PACE test | End-of-workbook assessment | Graded and recorded. Routed to moderation if the student is at or above the moderation floor grade |
| Final test | Full unit test | Graded and recorded, same routing rules as a PACE test |

<Note>
Self tests exist so students can check their own readiness before the graded attempt. They are never moderated and never count toward a student&apos;s average or PACE completion.
</Note>

## Entering a test score

Administrators, supervisors, and monitors enter scores from Grading &gt; Score Entry, using either single or batch entry. Each entry requires the student, the PACE, the test type, an integer score from 0 to 100, and a test date of today or earlier. The system rejects a duplicate entry for the same student, PACE, test type, and attempt number.

As soon as a score is submitted, Zion:

1. Looks up the student&apos;s grade level and learning centre.
2. Applies the school&apos;s configured pass threshold to determine pass or fail.
3. Routes the score to moderation or finalizes it immediately, based on test type and grade level.

A failing graded score (PACE test or final test, not self test) notifies the student&apos;s centre supervisors, monitors, and administrators that a retest is required.

## Grade-based routing to moderation

Self tests always finalize immediately and skip moderation entirely. For graded tests (PACE test and final test), routing depends on the student&apos;s grade level against the school&apos;s moderation floor:

- Below the moderation floor: the score finalizes immediately after entry, with the audit trail still recorded.
- At or above the moderation floor: the score enters Stage 1 of the moderation pipeline.

The moderation floor defaults to Grade 8 and is configurable per school from Grade 1 through Grade 12.

## The three-stage moderation pipeline

Scores at or above the moderation floor pass through three stages before they count toward a report card.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Stage 1: Entry">
    A score is entered by an administrator, supervisor, or monitor and sits at `stage_1_pending`, awaiting internal moderation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stage 2: Internal moderation">
    An internal moderator approves or rejects the score. Approval moves it to `stage_2_internal_approved`. Rejection requires a reason of at least 10 characters and returns the score to `stage_1_rejected` so the original grader can correct and resubmit it, which restarts the moderation clock.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stage 3: External moderation and finalization">
    An external moderator either approves the internally-approved score, which finalizes it as `stage_3_external_approved` and locks the row from further changes, or flags it for ACE accreditation review as `flagged_for_ace`, which holds it out of grade calculations until the flag is resolved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

By default, only administrators can act as internal or external moderators. A user can never moderate a score they entered themselves, and can never move a score from Stage 2 to Stage 3 if they were the Stage 2 moderator. See [Moderation queue](/features/grading/moderation) for the full workflow, including SLA alerts, moderator reassignment, and delegated roles.

## Retests

A failing graded test can be scheduled for a retest by an administrator, supervisor, or monitor, with a retest date of today or later. Entering the retest score chains it to the original attempt, increments the attempt number, and reapplies the same pass threshold and moderation routing as the original test. Retest attempts are capped, and once the cap is reached, an administrator must grant an override before another attempt can be entered. See [Retest management](/features/grading/retests) for scheduling, chaining, and override details.

## Compliance audit trail

Every grading action writes an entry to the audit log: score entry, adjustment, moderation approval or rejection, finalization, retest scheduling and completion, and administrator overrides, each with the acting user, their role at the time, a timestamp, and the previous and new values where relevant. Administrators can filter the log by date range, action type, learning centre, and student, export it as CSV, and generate compliance reports covering grade changes, moderation compliance, score adjustments, and rejected tests, or export a full accreditation bundle. See [Audit trail](/features/grading/audit-trail) for the full reference.

## Settings and thresholds

Administrators configure grading behaviour per school:

| Setting | Default | Range |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Pass threshold, Grades 1 to 4 | 90% | 80% or 90% |
| Pass threshold, Grades 5 to 12 | 80% | 80% or 90% |
| Threshold band boundary (grade cutoff for the higher pass band) | Grade 4 | Grade 1 to 12, must be below the moderation floor |
| Moderation floor (grade at or above which scores are moderated) | Grade 8 | Grade 1 to 12, must be above the threshold band boundary |
| Maximum retest attempts | 3 | 1 to 5 |
| Internal moderator role | Administrator only | Administrator, or delegated to supervisor or monitor |
| External moderator role | Administrator only | Administrator, or delegated to supervisor or monitor |

If the threshold band boundary and moderation floor are set to an incoherent pair (band boundary not below the floor), both settings fall back to their defaults.

A delegated moderator&apos;s authority is limited to the learning centres they are assigned to. Removing a delegation takes effect immediately.
