The PACE catalog is Zion&apos;s reference to the ACE PACE curriculum. It lists every PACE the school can assign to a student, organized by subject and level. Administrators, supervisors, and monitors browse it from Settings. It is read-only: you cannot add, edit, or remove a PACE from the catalog itself. To put a PACE in a student&apos;s hands, use [PACE assignments](/features/students/pace-assignments).

## Catalog scope

<Info>
Zion&apos;s seeded catalog spans PACE numbers 1001 through 1756, across 23 subjects and levels 1 through 12.
</Info>

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Number range | 1001 to 1756 |
| Subjects | 23 |
| Levels | 1 to 12 |
| Core subjects | Math, English, Science, Social Studies, Word Building, and others |

The exact subject list, and how many PACEs exist per subject and level, is visible in the catalog itself. Do not assume a fixed count per subject. PACE counts vary by subject and level, and the catalog is the source of truth, not a printed formula.

## Where to find it

Navigate to Settings, then PACEs, to open the catalog. Each row shows the PACE number, subject, and level. Selecting a PACE opens its detail page, which also lists every student in the org currently in progress on that PACE.

## Searching and filtering

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search by number or keyword">
    Type a PACE number or a keyword into the search field to narrow the list to matching PACEs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by subject">
    Use the subject dropdown to show only PACEs for one subject, for example Math or Science.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by level">
    Use the level dropdown to show only PACEs for one level, 1 through 12.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Combine filters">
    Search, subject, and level filters apply together, so you can narrow to, for example, Math PACEs at level 3.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## PACE detail page

Opening a PACE from the catalog shows:

- PACE number
- Subject
- Level
- Students in the org currently marked in_progress on that PACE

This detail page is where you confirm who is working on a given PACE before you decide whether to assign it to someone else.

## What the catalog does not do

<Warning>
The catalog is a reference, not an assignment tool. Assigning a PACE to a student, tracking completion, and enforcing subject progression happen on the student&apos;s own page, not here.
</Warning>

- You cannot create, edit, or delete a PACE from this page.
- You cannot assign a PACE to a student from the catalog. Open the student&apos;s detail page instead.
- Subject progression rules (a student must pass earlier PACEs in a subject before a later one is assignable) are enforced at assignment time, not shown as a rule engine in the catalog.
