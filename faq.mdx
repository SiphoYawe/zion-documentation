## Parent and student portals

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is the parent portal available now?">
    Yes. The parent portal is a read-mostly area where a parent sees a per-child view of progress, attendance, homework, behaviour, grades, and report cards, plus a way to book meetings with staff. See [Parent portal overview](/features/parent-portal/overview).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is the student portal available now?">
    Yes. A signed-in student gets a read-mostly view of their own homework, goals, grades, progress, attendance, calendar, activities, and notifications. See [Student portal overview](/features/student-portal/overview).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does a parent get access to a child&apos;s record?">
    Access comes from an accepted parent invitation linking the parent&apos;s account to a specific student, not from an email address matching a student record. A pending or rejected invitation grants no access. If a child is missing from a parent&apos;s dashboard, the school needs to check the invitation status.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can a student see another student&apos;s data?">
    No. Every student server action resolves identity from the signed-in account, so a student can only ever load their own record.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Meetings

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Who can book and manage meetings?">
    A meeting is an in-person conversation between a parent and their child&apos;s supervisor or monitor. Parents, supervisors, monitors, and administrators all work with meetings. Teaching staff accounts other than the assigned supervisor or monitor, and student accounts, do not have access to this area. See [Meetings overview](/features/meetings/overview).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What are the two ways a meeting gets booked?">
    A parent can claim an open slot that staff published in advance, which books immediately with no waiting on a response. Or a parent and staff member can negotiate a time back and forth as a request. See [Availability](/features/meetings/availability) and [Booking and requests](/features/meetings/booking-and-requests).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Billing

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Who processes payment for Zion subscriptions?">
    Zion bills schools through Polar, which acts as the merchant of record. Polar collects payment and remits tax in every market Zion sells into. Prices are quoted and charged in USD with no currency conversion applied at checkout. See [Billing overview](/features/billing/overview).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who manages billing inside Zion?">
    Administrators manage subscriptions from Settings, under the Billing tab. Supervisors and monitors see a read-only summary of the same status. Parents and students do not see billing screens, except for a notice if a school&apos;s subscription lapses.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Data import and export

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I bring in students and grades from a legacy system?">
    Yes, through the import wizard at `/admin/import`, administrator only. It reads a CSV, walks you through mapping columns, previews the change, and commits it as a single all-or-nothing operation. It imports three entity types: students, PACE progress, and grades. Attendance history is not an importable entity, since attendance data in most legacy systems is inconsistent to import automatically. See [Importing legacy data](/features/data/importing-data) and the [CSV templates reference](/reference/csv-templates).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if my legacy data needs more help than a self-serve wizard?">
    See [Migration concierge](/features/data/migration-concierge) for what a guided migration covers.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I get my school&apos;s data out of Zion?">
    The unified export center at `/settings/export` builds a downloadable bundle of your school&apos;s records. Each request produces per-entity CSV files plus a PDF snapshot and runs in the background. Administrators and supervisors reach this from Settings, under the Data Export tab. See [Exporting your school data](/features/data/exporting-data).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Behaviour

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What does behaviour tracking record?">
    Merit, demerit, and incident events logged against a category catalog that an administrator defines. Each event carries signed points, and Zion computes a running net balance and timeline per student. See [Behaviour overview](/features/behaviour/overview).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can log and see behaviour events?">
    Administrators, supervisors, and monitors log and review these events. Parents see the same balance and timeline for their own linked children, with private staff notes removed from what they can read.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Homework

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is a homework assignment in Zion?">
    A home-study assignment tied to a specific PACE page range, not a graded file upload. A supervisor or monitor assigns a page range from one of a student&apos;s active PACEs, the family marks it done at home, and the teacher confirms it was actually completed before it counts as verified. See [Homework overview](/features/homework/overview).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Report cards

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What goes into a report card?">
    A student&apos;s per-term data compiled into a single PDF: PACE grades, test scores, attendance, Goal Check completion, and parent-safe character notes. See [Report cards overview](/features/report-cards/overview).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can generate and distribute report cards?">
    Administrators generate report cards for any centre and manage templates. Supervisors and monitors generate and distribute report cards for their assigned centre or centres. Parents view and download the ones published to them.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Roles

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What roles exist in Zion?">
    Administrator, supervisor, monitor, parent, and student. &quot;Teaching staff&quot; is a directory label for supervisors and monitors together, not a separate role in its own right. See [Roles and permissions](/reference/roles-permissions).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What&apos;s the difference between a supervisor and a monitor?">
    Both are scoped to their assigned learning centres and both work directly with students and parents day to day, covering meetings, Goal Check, attendance, grading, homework, behaviour, and report cards for their centres. Consult [Roles and permissions](/reference/roles-permissions) for the exact breakdown by feature.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can a supervisor or monitor be assigned to more than one learning centre?">
    Yes. Everything a supervisor or monitor sees is scoped to the learning centres an administrator has assigned them to, whether that is one centre or several.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
