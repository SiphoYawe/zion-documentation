As a supervisor you enter PACE test scores for the students at your learning centre and, where your school has delegated the role to you, take part in moderating scores for Grade 8 and above. This guide covers both tasks.

## Entering test scores

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Score Entry">
    Navigate to Grading in the sidebar to reach the score entry page. Use the single-entry tab for one student at a time, or the batch tab to enter several scores in one pass.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the student and PACE">
    Search by student name or number. Only PACEs assigned to that student appear in the PACE dropdown. If a PACE is missing, check the student&apos;s assignments in Student Management.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the test type">
    Pick pace_test, self_test, or final_test. This determines how the score is routed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the score and date">
    Scores are whole numbers from 0 to 100. The test date defaults to today; it can be backdated but cannot be set in the future.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Zion checks the score against the student&apos;s pass threshold and shows pass or fail immediately. A duplicate entry for the same student, PACE, test type, and attempt is rejected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Pass thresholds and moderation routing

Zion checks a submitted score against the school&apos;s configured pass thresholds and routes it based on the student&apos;s grade level. Both the threshold band and the moderation floor are set per school by an administrator; the figures below are the defaults.

| Grade band | Default pass threshold |
|---|---|
| Grades 1 to 4 | 90% |
| Grades 5 to 12 | 80% |

A self_test is a readiness check. It always finalizes immediately and is never sent to moderation, regardless of grade level.

A pace_test or final_test is checked against the moderation floor, which defaults to Grade 8:

- Below the moderation floor: the score finalizes immediately and is available for grade calculations.
- At or above the moderation floor: the score enters Stage 1 of the moderation queue and is not final until it clears external moderation.

If a graded test fails, Zion notifies the student&apos;s centre supervisors, monitors, and administrators that a retest is needed.

## Taking part in moderation

By default only administrators moderate scores. Your school can delegate the internal moderation step, the external moderation step, or both to the supervisor role. If your school has done this, you will see the moderation queue for your assigned learning centres.

<Note>
You can only moderate scores at learning centres you are assigned to, and you cannot moderate a score you entered yourself. This separation-of-duties rule is enforced automatically.
</Note>

### The moderation pipeline

| Stage | What happens |
|---|---|
| Stage 1: pending | Score entered by staff, waiting for internal moderation |
| Stage 2: internal approved | A moderator approved the score, or rejected it back to the original grader |
| Stage 3: external approved | The score is finalized and locked, or flagged for ACE review |

If you hold the internal moderator role for your centre, you review Stage 1 scores and either approve them or reject them with a reason of at least 10 characters. A rejected score returns to the original grader as a rejected entry.

If you hold the external moderator role, you review internally approved scores and either finalize them (the score locks) or flag them for ACE review.

### Handling a rejected score

When your entry is rejected, it appears back in your score entry queue with the moderator&apos;s reason attached.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the rejection reason">
    Check what the moderator flagged before resubmitting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify against the test paper">
    Confirm the student, PACE, and score against the original paper.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resubmit the corrected score">
    The corrected entry replaces the rejected one and re-enters Stage 1. The moderation clock restarts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Internal moderation has an SLA clock that starts when the score is entered: within 36 hours is on time, 36 to 48 hours is approaching, 48 to 72 hours is overdue, and beyond 72 hours is escalated.

### Correcting a locked score

If a score has already cleared external moderation and is locked, administrators and supervisors can still correct it as a documented exception. Every correction writes to the audit log.

## Retests

If a graded test fails, you can schedule and later enter a retest for students at your centre.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Schedule the retest">
    Go to Grading &gt; Retests, find the failed test, and set a retest date. The date must be today or later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the retest score">
    Once the student has retested, open the same failed test and enter the new score. Zion chains it to the original attempt and increases the attempt count.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Retest attempts are capped, 3 by default, and the cap is configurable at your school. Once a student reaches the cap, you cannot schedule another retest until an administrator grants an override.
