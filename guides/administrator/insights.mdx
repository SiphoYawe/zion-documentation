Zion&apos;s analytics suite gives administrators four connected views of school performance: time-series trends, subject and centre breakdowns, termly-goal predictions, and pattern insights. Administrators see data across the whole organization. Supervisors see the same tools scoped to their assigned centre or centres. This guide covers how to read each view and turn a finding into an action.

## Where to find analytics

<Frame caption="The analytics index with trends, subject and centre breakdowns, and predictions">
  <img src="/images/guides/administrator/insights-1.png" alt="Zion analytics dashboard showing trends and breakdowns across the school" />
</Frame>

Navigate to **Analytics** in the sidebar to reach the analytics index, which links out to Trends, Subjects, Centres, Predictions, and Insights, plus a student picker for drilling into an individual record. The dashboard also embeds a small insights widget showing your top current insights, with a link through to the full Insights page.

<Note>
Pattern Insights is administrator-only. Supervisors can use Trends and Predictions for their own centres, but the Insights page is not part of their view.
</Note>

## Trends

The Trends view charts goal-achievement over time for a student, subject, or centre. Pick a window of 7, 30, 60, or 90 days, or the current term, and the chart updates accordingly.

From a student&apos;s trend detail page you can also see:

- Consecutive-misses highlighted directly on the chart
- A subject-level breakdown for that student
- A comparison against their centre&apos;s average
- An intervention notes panel
- A printable Student Progress Report

Use trends to confirm whether a pattern flagged elsewhere (a low centre score, a flagged insight) is showing up in the raw achievement data over time, not just in a single snapshot.

## Subject and centre breakdowns

### Subjects

The subject view shows goal-achievement by subject, a grade-band by subject matrix, a PACE-level completion-rate table, and a radar overview. Filter by date range (7 days, 30 days, or current term), grade band (1 to 4, 5 to 8, 9 to 12, or all), and subject. Export the filtered view as CSV or PDF.

### Centres

The centre view compares learning centres on goal achievement: mean and standard deviation across centres, outliers highlighted, a comparison chart, a top-5 and bottom-5 trend chart, and a full metrics table. Export as CSV.

<Tip>
Use the subject and centre breakdowns together. A subject that is struggling school-wide reads differently than the same subject struggling in one centre only, and the fix looks different too.
</Tip>

## Predictions

<Frame caption="The predictions page showing on track, at risk, and off track student cards">
  <img src="/images/guides/administrator/insights-2-predictions.png" alt="Zion predictions page with a donut summary and filterable student cards" />
</Frame>

Predictions project whether each student is on track to meet their termly PACE-count goal, based on their completion velocity so far this term. Every student and subject gets one of three statuses:

| Status | What it means |
|--------|----------------|
| **On track** | Projected PACE count meets or exceeds the termly target |
| **At risk** | Projected count is close to target, or progress is falling behind the expected pace for this point in the term |
| **Off track** | Projected count falls well short of the termly target |

The Predictions page shows a donut summary of on-track, at-risk, and off-track students, plus searchable and filterable student cards (by centre, status, and subject). Click a card to open a detail panel for that student and subject. Export the current at-risk and off-track list as CSV from **Export At-Risk Students**.

<Note>
Predictions use the same calculation the parent and student &quot;My Progress&quot; views use, so a parent or student sees the same status you do for their record. It is read-only for them.
</Note>

Early in the term, a student with zero completions is not automatically marked off track. Zion checks how far the term has progressed: less than a quarter of the term elapsed still counts as on track, less than half counts as at risk, and later than that counts as off track.

## Pattern insights

Pattern Insights runs six statistical detectors across the school&apos;s goal data and surfaces findings you might not notice by scanning trends manually.

| Type | What it looks for |
|------|--------------------|
| **Day-of-week pattern** | A weekday with achievement noticeably above or below the weekly average |
| **Subject correlation** | Two subjects where students&apos; performance moves together |
| **Centre performance** | A centre performing noticeably above or below the school average |
| **Seasonal trend** | A meaningful shift in achievement between the first and second half of the term |
| **Student velocity** | A student (or a meaningful share of students) accelerating or decelerating over recent weeks |
| **Subject difficulty** | A PACE with a low completion rate across many attempts |

Each insight carries a relevance score from 0 to 100 and a confidence level of high, medium, or low. Confidence reflects sample size: a finding based on a handful of goals is flagged low confidence even if the pattern looks strong, while a finding backed by dozens or hundreds of goals is flagged high confidence. Low confidence does not mean the finding is wrong. Treat it as worth watching rather than acting on immediately.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the insight">
    Read the title and description to understand what was detected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check confidence">
    Weigh the confidence level before committing to a major change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View affected students">
    Open the insight&apos;s detail drawer to see which students are involved, with their achievement rate and goals completed against goals attempted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Plan and take action">
    Decide on a concrete step: a curriculum adjustment, a targeted intervention, a conversation with a centre supervisor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dismiss when resolved">
    Dismiss the insight once you have acted on it or decided it does not apply.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Dismissing an insight is permanent. It will not reappear even if the underlying pattern continues, so only dismiss after you have acted on it or confirmed it is not relevant.
</Warning>

### Generating and refreshing

Insights regenerate automatically on a weekly cycle. You can also click **Generate Insights** to run a fresh analysis on demand, useful after a batch of new data or before a leadership meeting. Generating replaces the current non-dismissed set with a new one.

Insight generation, dismissal, and export require an active subscription on your organization.

### Filtering and export

Filter insights by confidence (all, high, medium, low) or by type (day-of-week, subject correlation, centre performance, seasonal trend, student velocity, subject difficulty). Insights sort by relevance score by default. Export a PDF of the full insight list, or export a single insight, from the Insights page.

## Building insight history

Pattern detection needs enough goal data to find a real signal. A school that has just started using Zion will see few or no insights at first. As daily goal data accumulates over several weeks, more patterns become detectable. Consistent daily goal marking across centres is what feeds this.
