School management covers the settings that shape how Zion behaves for your whole school: the school profile, academic terms, two operational toggles, and the alert and weekly report configuration. All of these settings are administrator-only. Supervisors and monitors do not see these pages, and non-administrators are redirected to their dashboard if they try to reach them directly.

## School Profile

<Frame caption="The school profile page with identifying information and behavior toggles">
  <img src="/images/guides/administrator/school-settings-1.png" alt="Zion school profile settings page showing name, contact details, and toggles" />
</Frame>

The school profile page (Settings &gt; School Profile) holds your school&apos;s identifying information and two behavior toggles.

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| School name | Required, 2 to 100 characters |
| City | Optional |
| Country | Optional, chosen from a dropdown of roughly 14 countries plus &quot;Other&quot; |
| Contact email | Optional, validated email format |
| Contact phone | Optional |
| Timezone | Defaults to UTC, chosen from about 10 preset zones |
| Language | Defaults to English, currently the only option offered |

<Note>
Saving the school profile is gated on the `settings:edit_org` permission, which is granted to the administrator role by default. See [Roles &amp; Permissions](/features/organization/roles-and-permissions) if you use custom roles.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open School Profile">
    Go to Settings &gt; School Profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the fields you need">
    Change name, city, country, contact details, timezone, or language.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the two toggles">
    Decide whether PACEs auto-advance and whether Ritual goals need approval, described below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Saving also marks the &quot;school profile&quot; step complete on the getting-started checklist if it was still open.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Timezone changes only apply going forward. Changing timezone does not retroactively adjust dates already recorded in Goal Check.
</Warning>

## The Two Toggles

The School Profile page includes two switches that change how Goal Check behaves across your school.

### Auto-advance PACEs on completion

Off by default. When a student completes a PACE, this controls what happens next.

- **Off:** the student sees a one-click suggestion for the next PACE in that subject. A supervisor or the student still confirms it.
- **On:** Zion automatically assigns the next PACE in the subject as soon as the current one is marked complete.

### Require goal approval in the Ritual

Off by default. This affects goals entered during the Morning Ritual.

- **Off:** goals set in the Ritual go live immediately.
- **On:** goals set in the Ritual start in a pending state and need sign-off from a supervisor or administrator before they count. Use this if you want a second set of eyes on Ritual goals before students act on them.

See [Rituals](/features/goal-check/rituals) for how ritual goal entry works day to day.

## Academic Terms

<Frame caption="The academic terms page listing terms with start and end dates and the active term marked">
  <img src="/images/guides/administrator/school-management-1-terms.png" alt="Zion academic terms page showing a list of terms with one set as current" />
</Frame>

Goal Check data is scoped to a term, so every school needs at least one before Goal Check data can be recorded. Terms are created during initial onboarding, and can also be managed from Academic Terms afterward.

- Each term has a name, a start date, and an end date.
- Only one term is active at a time. All new Goal Check entries go to the active term.
- Switching the active term does not delete data from the previous term. Historical terms remain available for reports and exports.
- Because switching terms changes where new data lands, do this on the actual first day of the new term, after final reports for the previous term are generated.

<Tip>
Create your terms for the whole academic year up front. You can add future terms without activating them, then activate each one when it starts.
</Tip>

For the full walkthrough of creating your first terms during setup, see [Onboarding](/guides/administrator/onboarding).

## Alert Settings

Alert Settings (Settings &gt; Alerts, at `/admin/settings/alerts`) controls how Zion flags at-risk students and lets you trigger alerts and digests on demand.

- **Generate Alerts Now:** runs alert analysis across all active students immediately, instead of waiting for the automatic daily run.
- **Send Digest Now:** emails unacknowledged alerts from the last 24 hours to all administrators, instead of waiting for the automatic daily send.

| Threshold | Default |
|---|---|
| Consecutive missed goals | 3 days |
| Declining trend | 20% drop over 2 weeks |
| Subject struggle | 60% miss rate, minimum 5 goals |
| Sudden drop | From 80% down to 50% within a week |
| Termly goal-at-risk projection | 80% |
| Centre low performance | 60% |
| Alert auto-expiry | 7 days |

Raise a threshold to make it less sensitive (fewer alerts, only clearer cases), or lower it to catch issues sooner. See [Alerts](/features/alerts/overview) for what each alert type means for the student it fires on.

## Weekly Report Settings

Weekly Report Settings (Settings &gt; Weekly Reports, at `/admin/settings/weekly-reports`) controls the automated weekly Goal Check report.

- Enable or disable the automated report.
- Choose the day and time it generates. Generation runs in your school&apos;s configured timezone, so set the timezone in School Profile first if it is wrong.
- Manage the recipient list. Recipients are persisted and can include people outside the administrator role.

For details on both settings surfaces, see [Alerts &amp; Reports](/features/school-settings/alerts-and-reports).
