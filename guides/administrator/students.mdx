This guide covers the four things an administrator sets up and maintains around students: the roster itself, CSV import, PACE assignment and progression, and academic terms. Supervisors and monitors can also add students, import CSV files, and assign PACEs, but they only see students in their own assigned learning centres. Academic term configuration is administrator-only.

## Roster management

Each student record holds a name, an optional student ID, date of birth, grade level (1 to 12), a learning centre, and optional parent email and phone. A duplicate student ID within your organization is rejected at creation.

<Frame caption="The student roster with centre and grade filters">
  <img src="/images/guides/administrator/students-1.png" alt="Zion students page showing the full roster with status badges" />
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the roster">
    Navigate to Students in the sidebar. Administrators see every student in the organization; supervisors and monitors see only students in their assigned centres.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a student">
    Click &quot;Add Student&quot; (or &quot;Add Your First Student&quot; on an empty roster) and fill in name, date of birth, grade level, and learning centre. Student ID and parent contact details are optional.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Submit the form. If you entered a parent email, a parent invitation is sent automatically. This does not block student creation if it fails.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame caption="The add student form with name, date of birth, grade level, and centre fields">
  <img src="/images/guides/administrator/students-2-new.png" alt="Zion add student dialog with roster fields filled in" />
</Frame>

To edit a student, open their record and update any field, including reassigning their learning centre or grade level. Deactivating a student is a soft delete: the student disappears from active lists and Goal Check entry, but all historical Goal Check, grading, and attendance data is preserved and can still be reported on. Reactivating restores the student to active lists. Reassigning centres mid-term works immediately but disrupts a student&apos;s routine with their supervisor, so plan centre moves around term breaks where possible.

### Bulk roster actions

Select up to 200 students at once from the roster to:

- Deactivate them in bulk
- Reassign centre and/or grade level in bulk
- Assign up to 12 PACEs to the group in bulk

Each student in a bulk action is evaluated on its own. If one student is blocked (for example, by a progression rule or an inactive status), that student is skipped with a reason and the rest of the batch still completes. One audit log entry is written per bulk action.

<Note>
Supervisors and monitors are restricted server-side to their assigned centres. If a supervisor or monitor applies an unassigned centre filter, they see zero results rather than the full organization roster.
</Note>

## CSV bulk import

CSV import is the fastest way to load a roster at the start of a school year or when transferring from another system.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the import dialog">
    From Students, click &quot;Import CSV.&quot; Download the template if you need the column layout.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your file">
    Upload a `.csv` file up to 5MB, with one row per student: name, student ID, date of birth, grade level, learning centre, and optional parent email and phone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validate">
    Click &quot;Validate CSV.&quot; Zion previews each row as valid or invalid and flags any learning centre that would go at or over its configured capacity. Capacity warnings are informational and do not block the import.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Import valid rows">
    Click &quot;Import valid rows.&quot; The server re-validates every row again at import time, so only rows that pass both checks are written.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fix and re-upload skipped rows">
    Download the error report for any skipped rows, correct the CSV, and re-upload if needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

See the exact column layout in the [CSV templates reference](/reference/csv-templates) and the full walkthrough in [CSV Bulk Import](/features/students/bulk-import).

## PACE assignment and progression

The PACE catalog is a read-only reference of the ACE curriculum: PACE numbers 1001 to 1756 across 23 subjects and grade levels 1 through 12. Browse and search it at [PACE Catalog](/features/paces/catalog).

A student needs PACEs assigned before a supervisor can set Goal Check goals for them. Assignment enforces one rule: you cannot assign PACE N in a subject until every earlier PACE in that same subject has been passed. &quot;Passed&quot; means a finalized passing score on a pace_test or final_test, the PACE already marked completed, or an administrator-granted progression override with a recorded intervention plan on file. A self-test score does not satisfy this rule. Assignment also fails if the student is inactive or already has that PACE assigned.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the student">
    From Students, click the student&apos;s name to open their detail page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign PACEs">
    Open the PACE assignment dialog, search the catalog by number or subject, and select one or more PACEs (up to 12 per assignment call).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Save the assignment. Inventory for each assigned PACE is decremented automatically. A low or negative stock count shows as a warning and does not block the assignment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

When a student finishes a PACE, mark it completed on their profile. If your school has the auto-advance PACEs setting turned on, the next sequential PACE in that subject is assigned automatically, still subject to the same progression, centre, and role checks. With auto-advance off, which is the default, Zion suggests the next PACE for a one-click assignment instead. A completed PACE can be reopened back to in-progress if it was marked complete in error, and any assignment can be unassigned entirely if it was made by mistake.

See [PACE Assignments](/features/students/pace-assignments) for the full assignment and progression walkthrough.

## Academic terms

Academic terms anchor Goal Check, grading, and reporting to your school calendar. Term configuration is administrator-only.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open academic terms">
    Navigate to Academic Terms.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a term">
    Click &quot;Create New Term&quot; and enter a name (3 to 50 characters), a start date, and an end date. The end date must fall after the start date, and a date range that overlaps an existing term is rejected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the current term">
    Click &quot;Set as current&quot; on the term that should be active now. Only one term can be current at a time. Setting a new current term clears the flag from every other term.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit or delete as needed">
    Deleting a term is blocked if it already has goals or scores linked to it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If no term is explicitly set as current, Zion falls back to whichever term&apos;s date range contains today.
