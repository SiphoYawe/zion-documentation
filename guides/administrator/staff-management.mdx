This guide walks administrators through the day-to-day work of staff management in Zion: inviting teaching staff, editing or deactivating accounts, layering custom permissions on top of built-in roles, and tracking whether parents have accepted their portal invitations.

<Note>
Zion has five account roles: administrator, supervisor, monitor, student, and parent. 'Teaching Staff' is a directory label for accounts holding the administrator, supervisor, or monitor role, not a role of its own.
</Note>

## Where staff accounts live

<Frame caption="The teaching staff directory with roles, centres, and an invite button">
  <img src="/images/guides/administrator/staff-management-1.png" alt="Zion teaching staff page listing administrators, supervisors, and monitors" />
</Frame>

Two pages list the same set of teaching staff accounts:

- **Teaching Staff**, reached from the sidebar under People &amp; Centres
- **Users**, reached from Settings &gt; Users

Both show every administrator, supervisor, and monitor account, each person&apos;s assigned learning centres, and an 'Invite Teaching Staff' button. The Users page additionally shows a Pending Invitations table with email, role, sent date, status, and Resend or Revoke actions for invitations that have not been accepted yet.

<Tip>
Create your learning centres before inviting staff. Monitors require at least one assigned centre at invite time, and supervisors are usually assigned one too.
</Tip>

## Inviting a teaching staff member

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the invite form">
    From Teaching Staff or Settings &gt; Users, click 'Invite Teaching Staff'.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter email and role">
    Enter the invitee&apos;s email address and choose a role: Administrator, Supervisor, or Monitor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign learning centres">
    Monitor requires at least one learning centre. Supervisor centre assignment is optional but typical, since supervisors use assigned centres for approvals and reporting. Administrator clears any selected centres, since administrators are not centre-scoped.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a welcome message (optional)">
    Add a custom message, up to 500 characters, to include in the invitation email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invitation">
    Zion sends an invitation email through Clerk, its authentication provider. The invitee sets a password and joins. The invite stays in the Pending Invitations table until accepted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Staff roles summary

| Role | Centre scope | Typical access |
|---|---|---|
| Administrator | All centres | Full access across the school |
| Supervisor | Assigned centres | Approvals and reporting for assigned centres |
| Monitor | Assigned centres (required) | Students in assigned centres only |

For the complete staff-directory workflow, including editing details and reading the Pending Invitations table, see <a href="/features/organization/teaching-staff-and-users">Teaching staff and user accounts</a>.

## Editing, deactivating, and reactivating staff

Open a user&apos;s detail page from the staff directory to edit their name, role, or centre assignments, and to view their account information, assigned custom roles, effective permissions, and recent activity.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deactivate">
    Click Deactivate on the user&apos;s detail page. This blocks login, removes the person from the organization, and preserves their historical data for audit. Confirm in the dialog before it takes effect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reactivate">
    Click Reactivate on a deactivated user. This restores their login, re-adds them to the organization, and restores their previous role.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reset onboarding (optional)">
    Use Reset Onboarding when re-training a team member. It clears their onboarding progress so the getting-started guide shows again from the start.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Changing a staff member&apos;s role or centre assignments does not apply until they log out and back in. Tell them before you make the change.
</Warning>

## Roles and custom permissions

Every built-in role (administrator, supervisor, monitor, student, parent) is mirrored as a system role in Settings &gt; Roles, so its exact grants are visible in the same list used for custom roles. Administrators can also create custom roles that add specific permission keys, grouped by area (Students, Goal Check, Grading, Attendance, Calendar, Reports, Settings, Roles &amp; Permissions, Inventory), on top of a user&apos;s built-in role.

<Note>
Custom roles are additive. Assigning one adds permission keys to what a user already has. It never replaces or downgrades a built-in role.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings > Roles">
    View every role in your organization: name, description, a System badge for built-in role mirrors, permission count, and member count.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create, clone, or edit a role">
    Click New Role to start from scratch, or Clone an existing role, including a system role, to make a customizable copy. Check the exact permission keys to grant. System roles cannot be renamed, edited, or deleted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign the role to a user">
    On a user&apos;s detail page, add the custom role under Assigned Roles. You can also bulk-assign or bulk-remove a role across many users at once from the Roles page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review effective permissions">
    The Effective Permissions card on a user&apos;s detail page shows the resolved union of their built-in role and any assigned custom roles, attributing each key to the role that granted it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A small number of permission keys are marked 'Coming soon' and disabled in the role editor because nothing in the app enforces them yet. For the full setup workflow and permission catalog, see <a href="/features/organization/roles-and-permissions">Roles and permissions</a> and the <a href="/reference/roles-permissions">role and permission reference</a>.

## Tracking parent invitations

Zion invites parents to the parent portal by email, tied to the parent email address on their child&apos;s student record. Administrators and supervisors can review invitation status and re-invite parents who have not yet accepted.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the parent invitations list">
    This page is not linked from the sidebar. Navigate to it directly at /admin/parents.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review invite status">
    Each student with a parent email shows an invite status: invited, accepted, or no email on file. Students already connected show 'Connected' instead of an invite action.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-invite one parent">
    Click Re-invite (or Invite, for a first attempt) next to a student to send or resend that parent&apos;s invitation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bulk re-invite">
    Select several students and click Bulk Re-invite Selected. A summary reports how many invitations were sent, skipped because there is no parent email on file, skipped for cooldown, or failed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
A student without a parent email on file cannot be invited. Add the parent email to the student record first.
</Note>
