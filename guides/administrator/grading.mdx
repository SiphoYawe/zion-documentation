Administrators own the moderation pipeline for graded PACE tests. Supervisors and monitors enter scores. For students at or above the school&apos;s moderation floor (Grade 8 by default), an administrator (or a delegated supervisor or monitor) reviews and approves those scores before they count toward records and report cards.

<Note>
By default only administrators can moderate. A school can delegate internal or external moderation to a supervisor or monitor role from the Moderation Queue page. A delegated moderator&apos;s authority is limited to the learning centres they are assigned to.
</Note>

## What gets moderated

A self-test never enters moderation. It is an ungraded readiness check that finalizes immediately and is excluded from grade averages and progression.

A `pace_test` or `final_test` score finalizes immediately if the student is below the moderation floor. At or above the floor, it enters the moderation pipeline:

| Stage | What happens | Result |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Stage 1: pending | Score entered by staff | Awaiting internal moderation |
| Stage 2: internal approved | An internal moderator approves or rejects | Approved scores move to external moderation; rejected scores return to the original grader |
| Stage 3: external approved | An external moderator finalizes or flags for ACE review | Approved scores are locked; flagged scores are held out of calculations until the accreditation body resolves them |

The same person cannot moderate a score they entered, and cannot perform external moderation on a score they already approved internally. This separation of duties is enforced on every action.

---

## Your grading responsibilities

### Daily

- Process the internal moderation queue.
- Check the external moderation queue for internally approved scores awaiting finalization.
- Address SLA alerts before they escalate.
- Review scores that came back rejected for resubmission.

### Weekly

- Check SLA compliance across both moderation stages.
- Review adjustment and rejection patterns by supervisor.
- Check the audit-integrity badge on the Audit Log page.

### Monthly

- Generate term grade reports.
- Compare centre performance.
- Export a compliance report or accreditation bundle if requested by leadership.

## Internal moderation queue

Navigate to **Grading &gt; Moderation Queue**.

The queue lists every score in stage 1, sortable by time pending, with per-moderator metrics and history. Each row shows the student, PACE and subject, score, pass or fail, who entered it, entry time, hours pending, and SLA status.

### Approving a score

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the score row">
    Review the entered value against the context you have (test paper, supervisor notes).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Approve">
    Add an optional note if useful for the record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    The score moves to stage 2 (internally approved) and is queued for external moderation. The action is written to the audit log.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Rejecting a score

Rejection requires a reason of at least 10 characters. The score returns to the original grader as `stage_1_rejected`. They correct and resubmit it from the Score Entry page, which restarts the internal SLA clock.

Example reasons: wrong student selected, score does not match the test paper, PACE not yet completed by the student.

### Adjusting a score

An administrator can change the value directly with `adjustTestScore`, also with a mandatory reason of at least 10 characters. The original value is preserved in the audit log and `score_adjusted` is flagged on the row.

<Warning>
A reason is required for every rejection and adjustment. This is what makes the ACE compliance audit trail complete.
</Warning>

## External moderation queue

Navigate to **Grading &gt; External Moderation** (`/admin/grading/moderation/external`).

This queue holds scores already approved internally (stage 2). An external moderator either:

- **Approves and finalizes**, which locks the score (`stage_3_external_approved`, `is_locked = true`) and writes a `grade_finalized` audit row, or
- **Flags for ACE review**, which marks the score `flagged_for_ace` and excludes it from finalized calculations until the accreditation body resolves it.

A moderator who internally approved a score cannot also externally approve it.

---

## SLA tracking

Zion tracks two separate SLA clocks. Internal moderation is checked in hours; external moderation, which typically involves outside reviewers, uses longer windows measured in days.

**Internal moderation (stage 1 to 2), clock starts at entry:**

| Status | Hours pending |
|--------|----------------|
| Within SLA | 0-36 |
| Approaching | 36-48 |
| Overdue | 48-72 |
| Escalated | 72+ |

**External moderation (stage 2 to 3), clock starts at internal approval:**

| Status | Hours pending |
|--------|----------------|
| Within SLA | 0-120 |
| Approaching | 120-168 |
| Overdue | 168-192 |
| Escalated | 192+ |

These windows are fixed in the system and are not configurable per school.

### Moderation alerts

Navigate to **Grading &gt; Alerts** (`/admin/grading/alerts`) to see active alerts grouped by escalated, overdue, and warning, plus a history of acknowledged alerts.

Acknowledging an alert does not clear the underlying moderation item. It records that you have seen it and still commit to processing it.

---

## Filtering either queue

Both the internal and external moderation queues can be filtered by learning centre, subject, grade level, and SLA status, so you can prioritize the students closest to their SLA deadline or focus a review session on one subject or centre.

---

## Reassigning a stuck moderation

If a score has sat unmoderated because the assigned moderator is unavailable, open the queue and reassign it to another active administrator, supervisor, or monitor. The system logs a `moderation_reassigned` audit entry and notifies the new moderator.

---

## Delegating moderation authority

By default, only administrators moderate. From the Moderation Role Settings card on the Moderation Queue page, you can delegate:

- **Internal moderator role** to supervisor or monitor
- **External moderator role** to supervisor or monitor

A delegated moderator can only act on scores from the learning centres they are assigned to. Removing a delegation takes effect immediately.

You can also hold a centre-scoped `grading:moderate` permission grant that authorizes internal moderation independent of role, configured under [roles and permissions](/features/organization/roles-and-permissions).

## Retest oversight

Navigate to **Grading &gt; Retests** (`/admin/grading/retests`).

A failing graded score notifies the student&apos;s centre supervisors, monitors, and administrators that a retest is needed. Staff can schedule a retest for today or a future date, then enter the retest score once it is taken. Each retest chains to the original attempt and inherits its pass threshold and moderation routing.

### Retest cap and overrides

The retest cap defaults to 3 attempts (configurable 1-5 from grading settings). Once a chain hits the cap and the student is still failing, entry is blocked until an administrator grants an override:

| Decision | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Authorize attempt** | Raises the cap by one for that chain, allowing another retest |
| **Approve progression** | Records an intervention plan so PACE progression treats the PACE as satisfied without a passing retest |

An override requires a reason of at least 10 characters and is logged as `admin_override`.

## Audit trail and compliance

Navigate to **Grading &gt; Audit** (`/admin/grading/audit`).

Every grading action writes a row to the audit log: score entry, adjustment, approval, rejection, finalization, retest scheduled or completed, override, and reassignment. Filter by date range, action type, learning centre, performed-by user, or student, and view a full history timeline for any score.

### Exports and reports

| Output | Contents |
|--------|----------|
| CSV export | The filtered audit log |
| All Grade Changes | Every score entry, adjustment, and finalization |
| Moderation Compliance | Approval and rejection actions against SLA |
| Score Adjustments | Every adjusted score with before and after values |
| Rejected Tests | Every rejection with reason and resubmission status |
| Accreditation bundle (ZIP) | Grades, moderation actions, roster, and term dates in one export |

The audit-integrity badge on this page shows whether the most recent scheduled tamper check passed, is pending, or found altered rows.

## Configuring thresholds and moderation floor

From grading settings you can set:

| Setting | Default | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Pass threshold, grades 1-4 | 90% | Must be 80 or 90 |
| Pass threshold, grades 5-12 | 80% | Must be 80 or 90 |
| Threshold band boundary | Grade 4 | The grade cutoff for the higher pass band; must stay below the moderation floor |
| Moderation floor | Grade 8 | Grade at or above which scores are moderated |
| Max retests | 3 | 1-5 in this settings page |

Changes apply to future tests, not scores already entered.

---

## Monitoring quality

Watch for patterns across supervisors: a high rejection rate can point to a training gap, and frequent adjustments can point to a data entry issue at a specific centre. The grading dashboard (`/admin/grading/dashboard`) breaks down average scores, pass rates, and moderation status by subject, centre, and grade level, and includes an at-risk-student widget and a needs-attention list.

---

## Reports

The dashboard&apos;s Export Report button and the Reports page (`/admin/grading/reports`) generate a term grade report scoped to the selected term and centre, for use with parents, leadership, and accreditation reviewers.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Queue not loading">
    Refresh the page, check your connection, and clear the browser cache if the queue still fails to load.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Adjustment or rejection not saving">
    Confirm the reason is at least 10 characters, the score is between 0 and 100, and your account has administrator access or a delegated moderator role for that centre.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="A supervisor disputes a rejection">
    Review the rejection reason and the audit trail together, discuss it with the supervisor, and have them resubmit the score if they agree. Escalate to leadership if it stays unresolved.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
