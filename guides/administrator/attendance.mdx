As an administrator, you oversee attendance for the whole school rather than marking it every day. Supervisors and monitors mark attendance for the centres they are assigned to. Your work is monitoring the dashboard, reviewing alerts, generating reports, and reading trends across centres.

<Note>
Administrators are the only role with access to the Reports page and to corrections on locked past dates. Supervisors and monitors can mark attendance and, for supervisors, view a dashboard, alerts, and trends scoped to their own centres.
</Note>

## Your attendance responsibilities

### Daily

- Review the school-wide attendance dashboard
- Check for new critical or warning alerts
- Confirm every centre has marked attendance for the day
- Follow up on anything urgent

### Weekly

- Review attendance trends and centre comparisons
- Work through the alert queue and acknowledge or dismiss open items
- Generate a weekly report if your school requires one

### Monthly or termly

- Generate a monthly or term report for leadership
- Review chronic absence patterns and how interventions are tracking
- Check whether alert thresholds still fit your school

1. Navigate to **Attendance &gt; Dashboard**.
2. Review school-wide attendance metrics for the selected day.

### Today&apos;s snapshot

| Card | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Total Students | All active students |
| Attendance Rate | Today&apos;s overall percentage |
| Present | Students marked present |
| Absent | Students marked absent |
| Late | Students marked late |
| Unmarked | Students not yet marked |

### Absent students and late arrivals

The dashboard lists absent students (name, grade, learning centre, and how many times they have been absent this week) and late arrivals (name, arrival time, and any notes entered when they were marked late).

### Centre breakdown

A chart compares attendance rate and student count across every learning centre. Click a centre to drill into its own dashboard view.

## Attendance alerts

Zion generates chronic absence alerts automatically. A cron job runs daily; you can also trigger generation manually as an administrator.

1. Navigate to **Attendance &gt; Alerts**.
2. Review the open queue, filterable by centre and alert type.

### Alert thresholds

| Level | Trigger |
|-------|---------|
| Warning | Consecutive school-day absences reach the warning threshold (default 3) |
| Critical | Consecutive school-day absences reach the critical threshold (default 5), or the 30-day absence rate exceeds the critical rate threshold (default 20%) |

Consecutive-absence counting walks backward through school days, skipping weekends and holidays, and stops at the first present, late, or unrecorded day. A student&apos;s alert escalates from warning to critical in place if the pattern worsens, and resolves automatically once the student no longer meets either threshold on a later run.

Each alert shows the student&apos;s name and centre, alert type, consecutive absence count, 30-day absence rate, and the list of absence dates.

### Acknowledging and dismissing alerts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the alert">
    Check the consecutive absence count, the 30-day rate, and the absence dates. Look for a pattern, such as the same day of the week or the same centre.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take action">
    Contact the parent or guardian, schedule a meeting, or make a referral as needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Acknowledge or dismiss">
    Click **Acknowledge** and enter intervention notes describing what you did. Notes are required. If the alert is a false alarm, click **Dismiss** instead and enter a reason.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Intervention notes and dismissal reasons are written to the audit trail. Keep them specific: they are your record for parent meetings, escalations, and compliance reviews.
</Tip>

### Alert thresholds and settings

Administrators can adjust the warning threshold, critical threshold, and 30-day critical rate for the organization from the school settings alerts page. See [Alerts and reports settings](/features/school-settings/alerts-and-reports) for how to change these values and how email notifications for new alerts are configured.

## Attendance reports

Report generation is administrator-only.

1. Navigate to **Attendance &gt; Reports**.
2. Choose a report type: daily, weekly, monthly, term, or custom range.
3. Select the date range and the centres to include (all or specific).
4. Optionally include Goal Check correlation data.
5. Click **Generate**.

### What a report contains

- Overview: date range, centres included, total students, overall attendance rate
- Present, absent, and late counts, with a day-of-week breakdown
- Centre comparison
- Chronic absence list and late arrival patterns
- Optional Goal Check correlation, showing students with both attendance and goal completion issues

Reports can be exported as PDF or CSV, saved to a report history you can revisit later, and shared by email. A weekly attendance report is also generated and emailed automatically.

Use reports for board meetings, for parent conferences (student-specific records), and for keeping compliance copies of chronic absence interventions.

## Attendance trends

<Frame caption="Attendance trends over a selected date range with a centre comparison view">
  <img src="/images/guides/administrator/attendance-2-trends.png" alt="Zion attendance trends chart showing present, absent, and late counts over time" />
</Frame>

1. Navigate to **Attendance &gt; Trends**.
2. Pick a range: 7 days, 30 days, current term, or a custom range. Optionally filter by centre or grade level.

The trend chart shows daily present, absent, and late counts and the attendance rate, along with a summary: average rate, best and worst day, and whether the trend is improving, stable, or declining.

Use the centre comparison view to spot outliers. If a centre performs well, look at what its supervisor is doing differently and consider sharing that practice. If a centre is struggling, investigate what is different there before assuming the cause.

## Marking attendance and correcting past records

As administrator you can mark attendance directly for any learning centre, for the current school day, the same way a supervisor or monitor would: select a date and centre, then mark each student present, absent, or late, or use bulk actions.

Attendance for any day other than the current org-local calendar day is locked to normal marking. If you need to change a past record, administrators can turn on correction mode from the banner shown on a locked date and edit individual student statuses. Corrections are written to the audit trail with a before and after value, separate from the normal daily mark.

Use direct marking to cover for an absent supervisor or in an emergency. Use correction mode to fix a data entry error after the fact.

## Escalation for chronic absence

<Steps>
  <Step title="Contact the parent or guardian">
    For a critical alert, contact them the same day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Document the contact">
    Record what was discussed in the alert&apos;s intervention notes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schedule a follow-up">
    Set a meeting if the pattern continues.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Involve the centre supervisor">
    Make sure the supervisor is aware and watching the student&apos;s attendance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Escalate if needed">
    Move to a formal parent meeting, an attendance improvement plan, or an external referral if interventions do not change the pattern.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

### A centre is not marking attendance

Check whether the supervisor or monitor is logged in and has access to that centre. Mark the day yourself if it is urgent, then follow up with the centre staff.

### Absence rate jumps suddenly

Check for a school event, weather, or transport disruption before assuming a data entry error. Confirm the numbers with the centre before communicating them further.

### An alert did not generate for a student who should qualify

Confirm the student is active (not deactivated) and that alert generation ran for the day. You can trigger generation manually as an administrator.
